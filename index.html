The Platform Manifesto

DESIGN
AGAINST
CONFUSION.

We champion a return to physical intuition and clear affordances. Good design is an act of communication where the object explains its operation through its physical structure, eliminating the need for instruction manuals. This 3D lamp embodies these principles as it tells you exactly how to use it just by its shape. The top directs light right where you need it, and the visible joints show you how to bend the arm to move that light. Its base keeps the lamp steady on your desk, while the cord exit shows you where to plug it in. When you use the switch, you feel a click so you know that the lamp has turned on or off.

Principle 00
Form Follows Function

"Form follows function" means an object's shape should be dictated by its purpose so it explains itself. Scissors are a perfect example: their finger-sized holes and visible blades show you exactly how to hold and use them the moment you see them.

object language

affordance

Principle 01
Affordance

Affordances are the visual clues built into an object's shape that show you exactly how to use it, like a computer mouse that fits snugly into the hand. When a product is designed well, its form speaks for itself, allowing you to understand its function instantly.

e.g. A door handle shaped like a bar invites pulling — its form communicates the correct action instantly.
Mapping

NATURAL
CONTROL

Principle 02
Natural control

Natural control happens when a product's dials or buttons are placed logically so that their functions are clear without needing instructions. By using familiar patterns, like having a hot water tap on the right and cold on the left, with their corresponding colours, the design speaks directly to your intuition.

e.g. Tilting a steering wheel left turns the car left — the control direction mirrors the real-world outcome perfectly.
